Polyaspartic Floor Coatings

The professional topcoat standard — UV stable, hot-tire resistant, cures in hours not days. Part of Crete Creations' complete 4-layer floor system.

High Solids Polyaspartic — The Professional Topcoat Standard

Polyaspartic is the UV-stable, chemical-resistant, fast-curing topcoat that defines the performance of our complete floor system. As the final protective layer over our 100% solids epoxy base and full flake broadcast, high-solids polyaspartic is what gives a Crete Creations floor its extraordinary longevity.

With 24+ years working with epoxy, polyurea, and polyaspartic systems, Arturo specifies the right chemistry for each project. Our standard system — moisture vapor barrier, 100% solids epoxy, full flake, high-solids polyaspartic — is optimized for the vast majority of residential and commercial projects. When specific conditions call for polyurea or a modified approach, Arturo adjusts.

Why Polyaspartic Beats Standard Epoxy Topcoats

☀️
UV Stability — Holds Its ColorStandard epoxy topcoats yellow and amber in sunlight within months. High-solids polyaspartic is UV stable — your floor holds its color indefinitely.
⚡
Fast Cure — Back in Service QuicklyWalk-on in 24 hours. Vehicle traffic in 72 hours. Standard epoxy topcoats require 5–7 days. Your garage is back in service in days, not a week.
🌡️
Hot Tire ResistanceHot tires pulling into a garage can soften and lift standard epoxy topcoats. Polyaspartic handles this — completely eliminating one of the most common failure modes of inferior systems.
💪
Flexibility Through Freeze-ThawMore flexible than epoxy — moves with the concrete through Missouri's extreme freeze-thaw cycles without cracking or delaminating.
🧪
Superior Chemical ResistanceMotor oil, gasoline, brake fluid, solvents — our polyaspartic topcoat resists all of them. Cleans up with a mop, doesn't absorb stains.

Part of a Complete System

The polyaspartic topcoat is one layer of four in a Crete Creations installation. It performs best when the layers beneath it are properly done — which is why we start with a moisture vapor barrier, use 100% solids epoxy for the base coat, broadcast flake at 100% coverage, and then finish with high-solids polyaspartic. The system works together. No shortcuts on any layer.
