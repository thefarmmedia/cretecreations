Epoxy is the right base coat — hard, dense, excellent adhesion to diamond-ground concrete. Polyaspartic is the right topcoat — UV stable (won't yellow in sunlight), hot-tire resistant, and faster curing. Arturo uses both: 100% solids epoxy as the base coat, high-solids polyaspartic as the finishing coat on every installation. Read the full comparison →

Day 1: diamond grinding and prep (4–8 hours). Day 2: base coat, flake broadcast, grout coat, polyaspartic topcoat (4–6 hours). Walk-on ready in 24 hours. Vehicle traffic in 72 hours. Full hardness in 5–7 days. Most two-car garages are fully complete in two days.

Moisture vapor is the biggest hidden cause of floor coating failure in Missouri. It moves upward through the slab from below — and over time it breaks the bond between the coating and concrete, causing bubbling and delamination. Arturo applies a moisture vapor barrier before every base coat. Most contractors skip this step entirely. Read why prep is critical →

A properly installed Crete Creations floor — diamond ground, moisture vapor barrier, 100% solids epoxy base, full flake, polyaspartic topcoat — should last 15–20 years under normal residential garage use. The biggest factor is preparation quality. Read what makes floors last →
