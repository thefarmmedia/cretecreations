What is your complete floor system?
Our standard system: (1) moisture vapor barrier applied first, (2) 100% solids epoxy base coat, (3) 100% full flake broadcast wall-to-wall, (4) high-solids polyaspartic topcoat. With 24+ years working with epoxy, polyurea, and polyaspartic systems, Arturo specifies the exact combination that fits your concrete's specific needs.
Why 100% solids epoxy and not regular epoxy?
100% solids epoxy contains no solvents or water carriers — every ounce you apply stays in the cured film. Solvent-based products shrink as the carrier evaporates, leaving a thinner, weaker film. 100% solids cures at full applied thickness — harder, denser, more durable. We use it exclusively as our base coat.
Why polyaspartic instead of epoxy as a topcoat?
Standard epoxy topcoats yellow in UV light, often dramatically within the first year. High-solids polyaspartic is UV stable, cures faster (vehicle-ready in 72 hrs vs. 5–7 days for epoxy), resists hot tires, is more flexible through freeze-thaw cycles, and has superior chemical resistance. No meaningful performance category where an epoxy topcoat beats polyaspartic.
What is a moisture vapor barrier and why does it matter?
Concrete breathes — moisture vapor moves upward through slabs. Over time, this pressure can break the bond between coating and concrete from below, causing bubbling and delamination. A moisture vapor barrier, applied before the epoxy base coat, prevents this failure mode. Especially important in Missouri's climate. Most contractors skip it. We don't.
Can you use different systems for different situations?
Yes — and this is important. After 24+ years with epoxy, polyurea, and polyaspartic, Arturo has the knowledge to specify the right combination for each specific floor. Moisture-heavy slabs, cold-weather installs, rapid commercial return-to-service — all call for different specifications. That expertise is what you're hiring.
How long does a garage floor installation take?
Typically two days. Day one: diamond grinding, crack repair, moisture vapor barrier. Day two: 100% solids epoxy base, full flake broadcast, grout coat, polyaspartic topcoat. Walk-on in 24 hours. Vehicle traffic in 72 hours.
What are spray-on driveway overlays?
A polymer-modified cement overlay applied over your existing driveway, creating a new surface finished in any of 11 styles — acid stain, brick inlay, stone scoring, stenciled patterns, custom medallions. Costs 40–60% less than full replacement, takes 2–3 days, and looks dramatically better than plain new concrete.
How much does an epoxy garage floor cost?
2025 ranges: single-car garage (~280 sq ft) $1,200–$1,800; two-car (~500 sq ft) $2,000–$3,200; three-car/shop (~700 sq ft) $3,200–$5,500. Metallic epoxy adds ~$1–2.50/sq ft. Driveway overlays and commercial projects quoted individually after on-site assessment.

Do you offer metallic epoxy?
Yes — one of Arturo's signatures. Every pour is one-of-a-kind. Metallic pigments move and interact as the epoxy cures creating organic, flowing patterns that can't be duplicated. Popular for luxury garages, restaurants, showrooms, and high-design residential spaces.
How do I maintain my coated floor?
Sweep regularly, mop with a pH-neutral cleaner occasionally, wipe spills promptly. The high-solids polyaspartic topcoat is non-porous and dramatically easier to maintain than bare concrete. For worn areas after years of heavy use, Arturo can apply a maintenance coat to restore the finish.
