Our standard system: (1) moisture vapor barrier applied first, (2) 100% solids epoxy base coat, (3) 100% full flake broadcast wall-to-wall, (4) high-solids polyaspartic topcoat. With 24+ years working with epoxy, polyurea, and polyaspartic systems, Arturo specifies the exact combination that fits your concrete's specific needs. Read more in our complete system guide.
100% solids epoxy contains no solvents or water carriers — every ounce you apply stays in the cured film. Solvent-based products shrink as the carrier evaporates, leaving a thinner, weaker film. 100% solids cures at full applied thickness — harder, denser, more durable. We use it exclusively as our base coat.
Standard epoxy topcoats yellow in UV light, often dramatically within the first year. High-solids polyaspartic is UV stable, cures faster (vehicle-ready in 72 hrs vs. 5–7 days for epoxy), resists hot tires, is more flexible through freeze-thaw cycles, and has superior chemical resistance. There's no meaningful performance category where an epoxy topcoat beats polyaspartic. Read the full comparison in our epoxy vs. polyaspartic guide.
Concrete breathes — moisture vapor moves upward through slabs from the soil below. Over time, this pressure can break the bond between coating and concrete from below, causing bubbling and delamination. A moisture vapor barrier, applied before the epoxy base coat, prevents this failure mode. Especially important in Missouri's climate. Most contractors skip it entirely. We don't. Learn more in our surface prep guide.
