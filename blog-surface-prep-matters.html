Why Surface Prep Is the Most Critical Step — And Where Most Contractors Fail

By Arturo Escalante · Crete Creations LLC · Joplin, MO · (417) 483-7082

The Uncomfortable Truth About Floor Coating Failures

When a garage floor coating peels, bubbles, or delaminates within a year or two, homeowners often blame the coating. In reality, the coating itself is almost never the problem. The problem is almost always what happened — or didn't happen — before the first drop of epoxy hit the concrete.

Diamond Grinding — The Professional Standard

Crete Creations uses diamond grinding as our primary surface preparation method. Industrial diamond-tipped grinding equipment mechanically removes the laitance layer (the weak, calcium-rich top layer on all concrete) and profiles the surface — creating the ideal mechanical anchor for the coating system.

Diamond grinding also removes oil contamination, reveals hidden cracks needing repair, and creates a consistent profile across the entire floor. Without it, the coating is essentially glued to a weak surface — and it will fail.

Why Most Contractors Skip It

Diamond grinding requires expensive equipment, skilled operators, and adds real labor to the project. Budget contractors skip it or substitute inadequate acid etching. Acid etching was the industry standard twenty years ago — it's been replaced by diamond grinding in professional applications because grinding produces measurably better adhesion and dramatically better long-term performance.

The Moisture Vapor Barrier

Even after perfect diamond grinding and crack repair, there's one more prep element most contractors skip: a moisture vapor barrier. Concrete breathes — moisture vapor moves through the slab from below. This pressure, over time, breaks the bond between coating and concrete. Our moisture vapor barrier is applied to every Crete Creations floor as the first coating layer, protecting the entire system from this invisible force.

When evaluating floor coating contractors, always ask about their surface preparation process. If they don't mention diamond grinding and a moisture vapor barrier, you have important information about the quality you'll receive.
