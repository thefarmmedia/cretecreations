How to Choose the Right Color, Flake System, and Finish for Your Garage Floor

By Arturo Escalante · Crete Creations LLC · Joplin, MO · (417) 483-7082

The Decisions You'll Make Before the First Coat Goes Down

A floor coating consultation with Arturo covers more than scheduling and pricing. You'll make design decisions that determine how your finished floor looks, feels, and performs. Understanding the options beforehand makes the process faster.

System Type

Full Flake Broadcast (Most Popular) — 100% coverage of vinyl flakes over the epoxy base coat, sealed with a high-solids polyaspartic clear coat. Hides surface variation, creates natural slip resistance, provides consistent appearance in dozens of color combinations, and hides dirt and tire marks far better than solid colors.

Metallic Epoxy — Metallic pigments create flowing, organic patterns completely unique on every installation. Luxury garages, showrooms, restaurants. Each pour is a one-of-a-kind piece of work.

Solid Color — Single uniform color without decorative flake. Clean and contemporary, occasionally specified for specific commercial applications or aesthetic preferences.

Color Selection

Gray and charcoal blends — most popular, clean, modern, hides tire marks beautifully. Black and white full flake — bold, high-contrast, photographs exceptionally well. Tan and beige blends — warmer, brighter, popular with brick and stone home exteriors. Custom blends — Arturo can adjust proportions for a combination specific to your space.

The most important note: colors look different under different lighting. Arturo brings a full physical sample set to every consultation so you see each blend in your actual garage, under your actual lighting.

Gloss Level

High gloss (most popular — showroom finish, easiest to clean), semi-gloss, and satin (occasionally specified for commercial glare reduction). High gloss is what most clients choose and what photographs the best in portfolio photos.
