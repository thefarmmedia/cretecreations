Epoxy vs. Polyaspartic vs. Polyurea: What 24 Years of Experience Tells Us

By Arturo Escalante · Crete Creations LLC · Joplin, MO · (417) 483-7082

The Chemistry Conversation Most Contractors Won't Have

Walk into most floor coating companies and you'll be sold one product. What you rarely get is an honest conversation about which chemistry actually fits your situation. Arturo Escalante has 24+ years of hands-on experience with all three — 100% solids epoxy, polyurea, and polyaspartic systems. Here's what he's learned.

100% Solids Epoxy — The Foundation Layer

Epoxy remains the right choice for specific applications — particularly as a base coat and primer layer. 100% solids epoxy provides exceptional film build and adhesion, high compressive strength, and compatibility with moisture vapor barrier systems. Where it struggles: UV exposure causes yellowing over time, cure times are long (5–7 days to full hardness), and application temperature windows are narrow. As a topcoat exposed to sunlight, standard epoxy is the wrong choice in 2025.

Polyurea — The Fast-Cure Workhorse

True polyurea systems can be walk-ready in under an hour — which creates unique application challenges but also unique advantages: extremely fast return to service, application possible in very low temperatures, and exceptional flexibility. Polyurea is Arturo's choice for cold-weather installations, rapid return-to-service commercial projects, and specific substrate conditions where flexibility is the primary requirement.

High Solids Polyaspartic — The Professional Topcoat Standard

Polyaspartic is a type of polyurea with modified chemistry that extends pot life (working time) to something manageable for floor application. This makes it the professional standard for UV-stable, chemical-resistant floor topcoats: UV stable, cures faster than epoxy, hot-tire resistant, excellent chemical resistance, and more flexible than epoxy through freeze-thaw cycles.

What Crete Creations Actually Uses

Our standard system combines the strengths of each chemistry: moisture vapor barrier, 100% solids epoxy base coat for maximum adhesion, 100% full flake broadcast, and high-solids polyaspartic finishing coat. When specific conditions call for polyurea — cold-weather installation, rapid commercial return-to-service, extreme flexibility requirements — Arturo specifies it. That's 24 years of product knowledge working for you.
