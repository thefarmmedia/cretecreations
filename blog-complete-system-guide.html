Our Complete Floor System: 100% Solids Epoxy, Full Flake & Polyaspartic Explained

By Arturo Escalante · Crete Creations LLC · Joplin, MO · (417) 483-7082

Why the System Matters More Than Any Single Product

When homeowners research garage floor coatings, they often focus on a single product — "epoxy" or "polyaspartic" — as if one coating determines the outcome. The reality is more nuanced, and Arturo Escalante's 24+ years of experience working with all three major floor coating chemistries gives Crete Creations clients something most contractors can't offer: the right system for your specific concrete, not a generic package.

Layer 1: Moisture Vapor Barrier

The first product applied to every Crete Creations floor is a moisture vapor barrier — and most contractors skip this step entirely. In Missouri's climate, moisture vapor moves through the slab from below, driven by humidity differentials and capillary action. This pressure, over time, can break the bond between coating and concrete from below — causing bubbling and delamination. Our moisture vapor barrier is applied to every floor as the foundation of the system, protecting every subsequent layer from this hidden force.

Layer 2: 100% Solids Epoxy Base Coat

Not all epoxy is equal. 100% solids epoxy contains no solvents or water carriers — every ounce you apply stays in the cured film. Solvent-based products shrink as the carrier evaporates, leaving a thinner, weaker film. 100% solids cures at full applied thickness — creating a harder, denser, more durable base layer with maximum adhesion to the diamond-ground concrete surface.

Layer 3: 100% Full Flake Broadcast

Decorative vinyl flakes broadcast at 100% coverage — wall to wall, no bare spots. Full broadcast maximizes surface texture for superior grip, creates consistent decorative appearance, and ensures the topcoat bonds to a complete, even flake layer. This is the professional standard — not the partial-coverage systems some contractors use to save material costs.

Layer 4: High Solids Polyaspartic Topcoat

The finishing clear coat is a high-solids polyaspartic — UV stable, chemical resistant, hot-tire proof, and flexible enough to handle Missouri's freeze-thaw cycles. This is what separates a floor that lasts 20 years from one that starts peeling in two. Standard epoxy clear coats yellow in sunlight. Our polyaspartic topcoat doesn't.

Epoxy, Polyurea, or Polyaspartic — We Use What's Right for YOUR Floor

Here's something most floor coating companies won't tell you: there is no single "best" coating chemistry for every situation. After 24 years of installing floors across every condition imaginable, Arturo has learned that the right system depends on the specific concrete and use case. Moisture-heavy slabs, cold-weather installs, rapid return-to-service commercial projects — all call for different specifications. Crete Creations is experienced with all three chemistries and uses each where it performs best.
